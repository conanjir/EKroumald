Designed by Conan Richards

Roumald's Typographic Dial ‼

EK Roumald is a typeface designed by Erkin Karamemet and released in 2024. It was published through Karamemet’s own foundry, making it effectively self-published rather than issued by a larger commercial house. Although some applications and showcases list the font as appearing in 2023, the official release year is 2024 according to publishing records.

The typeface is rooted in Anglo-American neo-classic forms, particularly those created by Linn Boyd Benton and later developed by Morris Fuller Benton around the turn of the twentieth century. References to designs such as Century Extended and Scotch are evident in the underlying structure, but EK Roumald does not simply replicate these precedents. Instead, it reframes them through a contemporary process-driven approach.

A central feature of the typeface is its method of conception. Karamemet employed a CNC router with a round drill bit as a “virtual drawing tool,” effectively treating the mechanics of machining as a way of sketching letterforms. This introduces a subtle technical character into the typeface and creates rounded counters reminiscent of the ink slurs found in small-type metal printing. The result is a serif display typeface that feels at once historical and engineered, sitting between traditional typographic forms and the tactile language of digital fabrication.

The conceptual rigour behind EK Roumald gives it a certain depth. However, it is worth noting that presenting the CNC process as a “virtual drawing tool” risks overstating its novelty. While the approach is evocative and poetic, the actual impact is stylistic rather than radically generative. The strength of the typeface lies less in its claim to process innovation and more in the way it conveys a tactile sensibility and precision that bridges revivalist serif traditions with contemporary design thinking.

The specimen is conceived as a dial of typographic time, a revolving register where letters, numbers, symbols, and ornaments orbit like the machinery of a clock. Instead of presenting characters in static rows, the circular arrangement foregrounds their rhythmic and mechanical qualities: rotation, spacing, and scale become integral to how the forms are seen. This reflects the design origins of EK Roumald, a typeface conceived through the mechanical process of CNC routing. The dial underscores that same sense of engineered repetition and precision, but also allows the eye to linger on the curvature and counters as they move, emphasising Roumald’s subtle mixture of revivalist serif forms and machine-driven texture.
